Croton

Family= Euphorbiaceae

order= Malpighiales

kingdom=Plantae

subfamiy=Crotonoideae

Croton is an extensive flowering plant genus in the spurge family, Euphorbiaceae.The common names for this genus are rushfoil and croton, but the latter also refers to Codiaeum variegatum.Croton is a diverse and complex taxonomic group of plants ranging from herbs and shrubs to trees.A well-known member of this genus is Croton tiglium.

Leaves:-The large,thick,leathery leaves are 2 to 12 inches long and quite variable.The shinny,alternate leaves may be linear to oval,havea smooth or lobbed margin.and some are wavy or twisted in to a spiral.the foilage color ranges from green variegated with white,pink,orange,red,yellow,purple in various combinations that may changes as the leaves age.

Flower:- When growing in the ground in frost-free-climates in spring insignificant flowers in long axillary racemes. The white male flowers with small petals and 20-30 stamens that gives a startburst-like effect are produced on separate inflorescences than the yellowish,petals-less female flowers.Pollinated Female flowers are followed by fruits which are small tripartite capsule about a third of an inch in diameter containing three small seeds.
